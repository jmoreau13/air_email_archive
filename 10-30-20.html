Good morning, 

This past June, I shared my thoughts about the social injustice faced by Black Americans. My intention was to be direct not performative. Race matters. And my first step was to understand and acknowledge the hand that Air and I play in the problem.

Step 2 is about transparency. The tech community is often the first to "champion diversity," yet we have disproportionately excluded the black community and other minority groups in hiring and advancement. From my perspective, we need to push for operational transparency so that we can be held accountable. Not after our 100th hire or next TechCrunch announcement. Now.

Starting today we will publish diversity metrics at Air. As you will find, this is clearly not a congratulatory note, it's the admission of a problem. We're putting our deficiencies on the table and taking the necessary steps to rectify them. We will update this data semi-annually and add levels of granularity as we grow

In addition to these metrics, we have also published all of our internal policies and practices. This amount of clarity will help us maintain an equitable and inclusive work environment. We will refine these documents over time, publishing updates quarterly.

At Air, we believe that it takes diversity of thought, culture, race, sexual orientation and outlook to build a world class business. Today we don't have this, and our commitment means nothing without action. I believe this level of transparency and accountability will force us to improve. Diversity is not happenstance, it's effort. Equity is not luck, it's clarity and planning. Inclusion is not reflection, it's critical evaluation, goal setting, and change.

If you have any feedback, comments, or thoughts please don't hesitate to reach out.
